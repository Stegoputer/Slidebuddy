Published by Brian Roemmele
17-21 minutes

In the ancient narrative from the Book of Genesis, Noah emerges as a figure of unwavering faith and preparation in a world on the brink of catastrophe. The story begins with a divine observation of humanity’s corruption and violence, leading to a decision to cleanse the earth through a great flood. Noah, described as a righteous man who walked with God, receives a direct command to construct an ark, a massive vessel designed to preserve life amid the impending deluge. This ark, built from gopher wood and sealed with pitch, measures approximately 300 cubits long, 50 cubits wide, and 30 cubits high, with three decks to accommodate Noah’s family, pairs of every animal species, and sufficient provisions.

For Such A Time As This

As Noah undertakes this monumental task, he faces ridicule and isolation from his contemporaries, who continue their ways without heed to the warning. The construction process spans years, during which Noah gathers materials, assembles the structure, and collects the animals as instructed. When the floodwaters arrive, bursting from the fountains of the deep and the windows of heaven, Noah and his charges enter the ark, where they remain safe for 40 days of rain followed by months of receding waters. This period tests their endurance, as they manage life within the confined space, tending to needs and maintaining order until the earth dries.

Upon the waters’ subsidence, Noah sends out a raven and then a dove to assess the land’s habitability, with the dove’s return bearing an olive leaf signaling renewal. The family and animals disembark to repopulate the earth, and God establishes a covenant with Noah, symbolized by a rainbow, promising never again to destroy all life by flood. This tale underscores themes of obedience, foresight, and rebirth, serving as a metaphor for navigating transformative crises through deliberate preparation and resilience.

Gather around the shared fire of our destiny. I present this not as a chronicler, but as a guide through uncertainty. The flood is coming. This is not a deluge of water, but a wave of transformation from AI, automation, and remade economies. This is the flood of the Abundance Interregnum, the passage we have charted across these 15 chapters, where old wage structures crumble under AI’s surge. In the next 5000 days, from late 2025 to 2039, you must become the architect of your future, the hero of your own journey.

We draw from Noah’s tale (a vitally important story no matter your faith), the archetype of preparation, and weave it through Joseph Campbell’s Hero’s Journey, the monomyth guiding our series. In Genesis, Noah lived in a corrupted Ordinary World, like our era of deskilled labor from Part 5: Your Deskilling. The divine Call to Adventure came: Make an ark. Noah crossed the Threshold into trials, gathering materials and enduring mockery. He entered the Inmost Cave of isolation, faced the Ordeal, and emerged through Resurrection to the Elixir of renewal.

This parallels our path. The 30 Pillars Of Resiliance

The waters rise as AI floods markets, ending jobs long marked for termination like COBOL coding but soon Python coding. You hold the blueprint. Here are 30 pillars, divided by realm, with paths and examples tied to our series. These are practical mandates to build Noah resilience. They will serve you in the times of the storms that we all will face. You will not be caught by surprise, you will be prepared.

The Mental Timbers: Fortifying the Mind Against the Waves (Pillars 1-6)

  1. Cultivate Unyielding Vision: Envision the Age of Abundance beyond the Interregnum, as in Part 1. Path: Set daily visualization sessions, journaling future scenarios where work is optional. Example: Imagine leading a guild from Part 13, creating value freely. This vision guides decisions, turning uncertainty into direction.
  2. Embrace Continuous Learning: Reskill as in Part 9’s Artisan’s Awakening. Path: Dedicate 30 minutes daily to online courses on platforms like Coursera or Khan Academy, focusing on AI tools or trades. Example: A former coder learns 3D printing, building prototypes for local makers, reversing deskilling from Part 5.
  3. Master Reframing: Reframe chaos as opportunity, from Part 4. Path: Use cognitive techniques like listing three positives per challenge. Example: When a job automates like in Part 15, view it as freedom to pursue passions, such as starting an AI-augmented side business from Part 10.
  4. Preserve Wisdom: Archive knowledge on SaveWisdom.org, as in Part 8. Path: Weekly uploads of skills or insights, collaborating with others. Example: A mechanic documents repair techniques, sharing in guilds from Part 13, ensuring expertise survives the flood.
  5. Foster Wonder and Curiosity: Rediscover awe, from Part 1’s consciousness call. Path: Engage in weekly explorations, like stargazing or experimenting with new tech. Example: Use AI to simulate historical events, sparking ideas for personal projects that blend old wisdom with new tools.
  6. Develop Strategic Foresight: Anticipate disruptions, from Part 14. Path: Create scenario plans quarterly, using tools like SWOT analysis. Example: Map job market shifts, investing in skills like robotics maintenance, as allies in Part 3’s historical lessons suggest.

These mental pillars form your keel. They provide stability to navigate changes. They will make you a safe place in the storm. And it is imperative you build these pillars today. The economics and the politics will work themselves through like they always do with crayons and no draftsman with fine tools. However your inner strength, this is not going to be built without a draftsman and builder. From your heart to your mind to your soul you must build your foundations here first.

The Emotional Anchors: Steadying the Heart in Turbulent Seas (Pillars 7-12)

  1. Heal Foundational Wounds: Confront traumas via therapy, from Part 2 and Part 6. Path: Seek professional help or journaling prompts to process grief. Example: A laid-off worker addresses loss anxiety, gaining strength to join communities from Part 13.
  2. Build Resilience Through Adversity: Embrace failures as ordeals, from Part 7. Path: Reflect on setbacks weekly, noting lessons. Example: After a failed venture, pivot to AI collaboration, as in Part 10, turning ordeal into growth.
  3. Nurture Gratitude and Meaning: Seek purpose beyond pay, inspired by Frankl in Part 2. Path: Daily gratitude lists tied to personal values. Example: Appreciate family time freed by automation, finding meaning in artisan crafts from Part 9.
  4. Practice Emotional Intelligence: Use empathy with AI, from Part 10. Path: Role-play scenarios or read books like Goleman’s Emotional Intelligence. Example: In team settings, mediate conflicts, enhancing guild dynamics from Part 13.
  5. Embrace Solitude and Reflection: Mirror Noah’s isolation with meditation, from Part 5. Path: Set aside quiet time for mindfulness apps. Example: Reflect on deskilling to rediscover strengths, leading to innovative reversals in Part 11.
  6. Cultivate Optimism Amid Chaos: See purification in the flood, from Part 14. Path: Consume positive media and affirmations. Example: View economic shifts as paths to abundance, motivating profit strategies from Part 12.

These anchors hold you steady. They prevent emotional drift in storms.

The Relational Bonds: Crewing Your Ark with Kin and Kindred (Pillars 13-18)

  1. Forge Deep Family Ties: Prioritize household, from Part 1’s plan. Path: Schedule shared activities, discussing future visions. Example: Involve family in learning AI tools, building collective resilience against job losses.
  2. Build Guilds and Communities: Form alliances, from Part 13. Path: Join or start online forums or local meetups. Example: A group of artisans collaborates on projects, sharing resources like in Part 9.
  3. Seek Mentors and Allies: Find guides, from Part 3’s Vonnegut. Path: Network on LinkedIn or attend workshops. Example: A mentor advises on AI integration, aiding obsolescence reversal from Part 11.
  4. Practice Radical Empathy: Understand plights, from Part 12. Path: Active listening exercises in conversations. Example: Support peers in chaos, strengthening networks for mutual aid.
  5. Nurture Networks of Support: Connect diversely, from Part 11. Path: Attend events or use apps like Meetup. Example: Build contacts in emerging fields, accessing opportunities in the Interregnum.
  6. Resolve Conflicts with Grace: Maintain harmony, from Part 6. Path: Learn conflict resolution techniques. Example: In family or guild disputes, use mediation to foster unity.

These bonds are your rigging. They connect parts for collective strength.

The Financial Planks: Securing Wealth for the Voyage (Pillars 19-24)

  1. Diversify Income Streams: Build multiples, from Part 12. Path: Start side hustles like freelancing or investments. Example: Combine AI content creation with trade services, buffering against shocks.
  2. Invest in Future-Proof Assets: Bet on AI or renewables, from Part 10. Path: Research via books or apps like Robinhood. Example: Buy stocks in automation firms, profiting from trends in Part 15.
  3. Practice Frugality and Stewardship: Live below means, from Part 4. Path: Track expenses with apps like Mint. Example: Cut non-essentials, saving for emergencies amid chaos.
  4. Leverage AI for Prosperity: Use tools to create value, from Part 15. Path: Experiment with platforms like Grok for business ideas. Example: Automate tasks to free time for high-value work.
  5. Build Emergency Reserves: Save six months’ worth, from Part 14. Path: Automate transfers to savings accounts. Example: Weather a layoff by drawing on reserves while reskilling.
  6. Plan for Legacy and Generosity: Think beyond self, from Part 8. Path: Set up wills or donate to causes. Example: Share wisdom profits with communities, seeding broader abundance.

These planks form your deck. They support steps through financial waves.

The Physical Beams: Tempering the Body for Endurance (Pillars 25-30)

  1. Prioritize Health and Vitality: Exercise and nourish, from Part 4. Path: Follow routines like walking or balanced diets. Example: Maintain energy for learning new trades in Part 9.
  2. Master Practical Skills: Learn repairs, from Part 9. Path: Take hands-on classes or YouTube tutorials. Example: Fix home appliances, reducing costs in frugal living.
  3. Cultivate Endurance Through Routine: Build stamina, from Part 6. Path: Consistent workouts like running. Example: Endure long hours in guild projects.
  4. Connect with Nature: Ground outdoors, from Part 1. Path: Hike or garden weekly. Example: Draw inspiration for creative reversals.
  5. Prepare Emergency Kits: Stock supplies, from Part 14. Path: Assemble kits with food, water, tools. Example: Use in disruptions like power outages from chaos.
  6. Pursue Holistic Wellness: Integrate body and mind. Path: Yoga or integrated health apps. Example: Achieve balance for sustained hero’s journey.
